The Founder Story

From Brokenness
to Becoming

There was a season of confusion, shame, unhealthy patterns, and living for outward performance. A season where identity was buried under expectation and pain.

Then came a turning point. A surrender. An inward healing that changed everything. From that place of restored identity and discovered purpose, You Inspire Now was born — not as a polished program, but as a personal mission.

"What I found on the other side of surrender, I needed to pass on. No woman should have to find her way through the dark alone."
🌸

The Heart Behind the Mission

You Inspire Now is not a program. It is a presence — a trusted space where women discover that their past does not erase their purpose, and their healing is possible, real, and worth every step.

Our Foundation

Everything we do flows from these pillars. They are not programs — they are the heartbeat of a movement designed to meet women exactly where they are.

Pillar One

Identity

Before anything else — before purpose, before community, before the next step — you have to know who you are. Not who the world says. Not who your past has told you. Who you truly are.

You Inspire Now exists to help women strip away the performance, the shame, and the confusion — and come home to their God-given identity. Value that cannot be earned and cannot be taken away.

You are not your mistakes You are not your worst day You are seen and known

Pillar Two

Purpose

You were not created to drift. You were made with intention, given gifts the world needs, and called toward an assignment that only you can carry. Purpose is not a destination — it is the direction you move when you know who you are.

This pillar invites women to stop waiting for perfect conditions and start moving toward the life they were created for — one courageous step at a time.

Your calling is real You were made with intention

Pillar Three

Resilience

Resilience is not pretending the pain did not happen. It is allowing honest healing to take place — and then rising anyway. It is the courage to take the next step even when everything in you wants to stop.

We build resilience by honoring struggle without glorifying victimhood — by saying "this was hard" and also "I am still here, and I am still becoming."
